the Urgent Need for Recycling

Harnessing the Power of Recycling for Environmental Protection

Plastic and polystyrene, pervasive in our lives, are major contributors to climate change, necessitating immediate action for environmental protection. These materials have severe ecological consequences: plastic's non-biodegradability pollutes oceans, landfills, and the air, while polystyrene poses significant environmental risks. Both emit greenhouse gases during production and incineration, exacerbating global warming.

Why Recycle?

The Crucial Role of Recycling

Recycling plays a vital role in safeguarding the environment. It reduces waste sent to landfills, conserves resources, and curbs greenhouse gas emissions. Recycling one ton of plastic saves approximately 2,000 pounds of oil and prevents the release of nearly 3,000 pounds of carbon dioxide. By recycling polystyrene, we reduce its impact on marine life and prevent the release of toxic chemicals during its decomposition.

To secure a sustainable future, embracing recycling programs and advocating for responsible manufacturing and consumption are essential. Together, we can harness the power of recycling to mitigate the threat of plastic and polystyrene, preserving our planet's biodiversity and ensuring the well-being of generations to come.

Paper

  • Paper-based materials that are clean
    and free from food residue or grease
  • (eg., Newspapers, magazines, office paper, envelopes, cardboard boxes, and packaging)
  • Laminated paper or cardboard (e.g., laminated posters or packaging)
  • Soiled paper products (e.g., paper towels, napkins) that cannot be effectively cleaned
  • Wax-coated or plastic-coated paper products (e.g., milk cartons, some take-out containers)

    • Plastics

  • Look for the recycling symbol with a number between 1 and 7 inside
  • Plastic bottles (water bottles, soda bottles), containers, and packaging
  • Soft plastics (e.g., plastic bags, cling film, chip packets) unless specified by local recycling programs
  • Plastic items contaminated with food waste
  • Polystyrene foam (Styrofoam) unless specified by specialized recycling facilitie

    • Cans

  • Aluminum cans, steel cans and clean metal food container
  • Metal items that contain hazardous materials
    (e.g., paint cans with leftover paint, aerosol cans with hazardous contents)

    • Glass Bottles

  • Glass bottles and jars
    including clear, green, and brown glass containers used for beverages, food products, and condiments.
  • Broken glass or glassware (e.g., drinking glasses, mirrors, window glass)
  • Ceramics, Pyrex, or heat-resistant glass

    • Food wastes

  • No organic waste can be recycled.
  • Organic waste is typically composted rather than recycled - Food scraps, garden waste, and other organic materials
